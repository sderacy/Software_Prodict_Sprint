Projects

These are the list of projects that I've worked on:

  1. Bookfinder App
    • This app is my current research project. We are currently working on a mobile application help students find books in the college library. I've worked on both the front-end and back-end of this project. I spent a lot of time working on the user interface because I personally enjoy working on the front-end but I have recently been devising a way to optimize the algorithm used to search for the book in the database. It's not an understatement to say this has been one of the larger sources of enjoyment of my college experience. Maybe SPS will be a contender!
  2. Montclair Excel Script
    • I know a guy who knows a guy who knows a guy who knows a guy at Montclair...*ahem*. All jokes aside, one of my friends from highschool is currently doing research at Montclair and he was complaining about he'd have to go through 400+ rock samples and manually enter 20+ columns of data. This was over the break and since I love helping others, especially my friends, I offered to create a python script that would do this process for him!
  3. ACT Peace Forum
    • A teacher from the Math and Science department reached out to the CS department asking to have a website built. At the time I was looking for ways to get more experience with website design, so I took up the project and helped Dr. Wang create a simple website that would allow him to post blogs related to the coronavirus.
  4. Text Summarizer
    • This one was done during a hackathon. I was a freshman at the time, and I worked with a sophomore and another freshman to create a simple Android app that determined the accuracy of one's summary compared to another source of text. It was a fun experience and I worked mainly on the front-end part of the app whereas my partners worked on the algorithm needed to grade the summarizer. Learning how to scrape websites was super fun!
